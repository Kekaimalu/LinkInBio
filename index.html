Media (& News) Literacy

Awareness of media influence and an active stance towards both consuming & creating media. To be proactive in combatting Misinformation & Disinformation, the tools of Media Literacy can help.

Step 1

Take control of your digital experience by conducting a scan of how and where you consume information. Be aware of the mediums (TV, Social Media, Newspaper, etc) you use to gather information. If the information is being filtered through personal opinions or accounts like Social Media search for the Real Source of Information.

Step 2

Take a step back and question your reactions to the things you see online. Disinformation outlets and accounts thrive on engagements–like and shares–on social media platforms. They're writing headlines designed to encourage you to spread their posts, even if they're false or misleading. Before taking the bait, question what you see.

Step 3

Understand what you're seeing. Distiguish between News and Opinion. Some stories, posts, or articless look like News but are actually opinion pieces. Is it News? Is it an Opinion? Before sharing or forwarding, consider the type of content first.

Step 4

Before you share take two step: First, Check the credibility of the source. Is the source Ethical? Meaning, accurate, check many viewpoints, fair, and objective. Is it Quality? Meaning, the source is willing to make up for mistakes and correct any misinformation quickly. Does it have Standards? Maintains calm and avoids emotions and idetifies sources/evidence.

Second, Fact check what you're reading. Unsure of how true a story is? Run it through Google or another search engine like Ecosia, Bing, or DuckDuckGo alongside terms like, "true," "false," or "hoax"
